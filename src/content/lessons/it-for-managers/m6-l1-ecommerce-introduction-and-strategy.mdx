## What Is eCommerce?

**eCommerce** (electronic commerce) is the buying and selling of goods and services over the internet. It encompasses not just the transaction itself but the entire digital customer journey: discovery, evaluation, purchase, fulfillment, and post-sale service.

For managers, eCommerce is not just a technology decision — it is a business model decision with technology implications at every step.

---

## eCommerce Business Models

| Model | Description | Examples |
|---|---|---|
| **B2C** (Business to Consumer) | Sell directly to individual customers | Amazon, Nike, most retail stores |
| **B2B** (Business to Business) | Sell to other businesses | Grainger, Alibaba, SaaS subscriptions |
| **C2C** (Consumer to Consumer) | Platform enabling consumers to sell to each other | eBay, Etsy, Facebook Marketplace |
| **D2C** (Direct to Consumer) | Brand sells directly, bypassing retailers | Warby Parker, Dollar Shave Club |
| **Subscription** | Recurring billing for ongoing access | Netflix, Birchbox, SaaS tools |

Knowing your model shapes every platform, pricing, and logistics decision you make.

## Platform Options: Build vs. Buy vs. Rent

| Approach | Description | Best For |
|---|---|---|
| **Custom build** | Developers build a bespoke storefront | Large enterprises with unique requirements |
| **Open-source platform** | WordPress + WooCommerce, Magento | Organizations that want control and have developer resources |
| **SaaS platform** | Shopify, BigCommerce, Squarespace | Fast launch, lower maintenance burden |
| **Marketplace** | Sell on Amazon, Etsy, eBay | Reach existing audiences, lower setup cost |

Most small and mid-size businesses start with a SaaS platform (especially Shopify) and add customization as they grow.

## Strategic Decisions Before Launch

Before selecting any technology, answer these strategic questions:

1. **Who is your customer?** — B2B and B2C customers have very different expectations for catalog size, pricing, checkout flow, and payment terms.
2. **What is your fulfillment model?** — Ship yourself, dropship, or use a 3PL (third-party logistics provider)?
3. **How will customers find you?** — Organic search (SEO), paid advertising, social, email, or marketplace?
4. **What are your margin requirements?** — Marketplace fees (Amazon takes 8–15%), payment processing (Stripe/PayPal take ~2.9%), and platform fees all compress margins.
5. **What regulations apply?** — Sales tax nexus, international customs, age-restricted products, and data privacy (GDPR, CCPA) all vary by product and market.

<Pitfall title="Launching Without a Traffic Plan">

Many organizations spend months perfecting their online store and launch to silence. A storefront with no traffic generates no revenue. Marketing strategy — SEO, paid search, email, social, influencer — must be planned *before* launch, not after. Budget for marketing from day one, not as an afterthought.

</Pitfall>

## Metrics That Matter in eCommerce

As a manager, these are the KPIs that reveal the health of your eCommerce operation:

| Metric | What It Measures |
|---|---|
| **Conversion rate** | % of visitors who complete a purchase (industry avg: 1–3%) |
| **Average order value (AOV)** | Revenue per transaction |
| **Customer acquisition cost (CAC)** | Marketing spend ÷ new customers acquired |
| **Customer lifetime value (CLV)** | Total revenue expected from one customer over time |
| **Cart abandonment rate** | % of carts started but not purchased (industry avg: ~70%) |
| **Return rate** | % of orders returned (high rates signal product or expectation issues) |

<Quiz question="Your company sells industrial safety equipment. Leadership wants to launch an eCommerce channel. A colleague suggests building on Shopify immediately. What strategic questions should be answered before committing to a platform?">

<div>

Before platform selection: (1) **B2B vs. B2C model** — industrial safety equipment is likely B2B, which means the platform needs to support purchase orders, net-30 payment terms, tiered pricing by account, and bulk ordering. Shopify can handle this but requires apps and customization. B2B-first platforms like Magento or BigCommerce B2B may be better fits. (2) **Catalog complexity** — how many SKUs, variants, and regulatory compliance documents need to be displayed? (3) **Integration requirements** — does the platform need to sync with your existing ERP or inventory system? (4) **Fulfillment model** — are orders shipped from your warehouse, or do you use a distributor? (5) **Regulatory compliance** — some safety equipment has certification requirements that must be surfaced in the product listing. Answering these before choosing a platform avoids expensive rebuilds.

</div>
