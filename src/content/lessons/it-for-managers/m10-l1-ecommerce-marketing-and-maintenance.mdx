## The Two Pillars: Marketing and Maintenance

A successful online store requires ongoing investment in two areas that managers often underestimate:

1. **Marketing** — continuously bringing new and returning customers to the store
2. **Maintenance** — keeping the store fast, secure, and operational

Building the store is a one-time project. Running it is an ongoing operation.

---

## eCommerce Marketing Channels

### Search Engine Optimization (SEO)
Organic search is the highest-ROI long-term channel. For eCommerce:
- Optimize product titles and descriptions with keywords buyers actually search
- Create category pages and blog content that answers pre-purchase questions
- Build backlinks through press, partnerships, and content marketing
- Ensure technical SEO: fast load times, mobile-friendliness, clean URLs, structured data (schema markup for products)

### Paid Search (Google Shopping & Search Ads)
Google Shopping ads show product images, prices, and store names directly in search results. They are particularly effective for product-intent searches (e.g., "buy waterproof hiking boots size 10").

### Email Marketing
Email consistently delivers the highest ROI of any eCommerce marketing channel (~$36 return per $1 spent). Key automated flows:
- **Welcome series** — introduces new subscribers to your brand
- **Abandoned cart** — recovers customers who left without purchasing (typically 10–15% recovery rate)
- **Post-purchase** — review requests, cross-sells, loyalty programs
- **Win-back** — re-engages lapsed customers

### Social Media and Paid Social
- **Organic social** builds brand awareness and community but rarely drives direct revenue at scale
- **Paid social** (Meta/Facebook Ads, Instagram, TikTok) is effective for awareness and retargeting, especially for visual products
- **Influencer marketing** works well for consumer products with clear lifestyle fit

### Retargeting
Show ads to visitors who came to your site but did not purchase. Retargeting typically converts 3–5x better than cold traffic and is a high-efficiency use of ad budget.

## Key Marketing Metrics

| Metric | Target / Benchmark |
|---|---|
| **Email open rate** | 20–30% (varies by industry) |
| **Abandoned cart recovery rate** | 5–15% |
| **ROAS (Return on Ad Spend)** | 3–5x is common target |
| **Cost per acquisition (CPA)** | Should be below customer lifetime value |
| **Organic traffic growth** | Month-over-month increase in non-paid sessions |

<Pitfall title="Treating Marketing as a Launch Activity">

Many organizations invest in marketing around the store launch and then reduce spending once the site is live. eCommerce marketing is an ongoing operation — SEO takes months to compound, email lists require consistent nurturing, and ad campaigns need continuous optimization. Treat marketing as a recurring operational budget line, not a project cost.

</Pitfall>

## eCommerce Maintenance: What Managers Must Own

### Security Updates
- Update WordPress core, WooCommerce, themes, and all plugins regularly — ideally weekly
- Use a staging environment to test updates before applying to the live store
- Enable automatic security updates for minor releases
- Run a security scanner (Wordfence, Sucuri) to detect vulnerabilities and malware

### Backups
- **Daily automated backups** of both the database and files
- Store backups in a separate location from the server (offsite or cloud storage)
- **Test restores periodically** — a backup that has never been restored is an untested assumption
- Define your Recovery Time Objective (RTO): how long can the store be down before significant revenue loss?

### Performance Monitoring
- Monitor uptime with a tool like UptimeRobot (free) or Pingdom
- Set alerts for downtime so you are notified immediately, not hours later
- Review page speed monthly using Google PageSpeed Insights
- Monitor database size and clean up post metadata, order data, and logs on a schedule

### Compliance and Policy Reviews
- Review and update shipping, return, and privacy policies annually or when regulations change
- Ensure your store meets current accessibility standards (WCAG 2.1)
- If selling internationally, review VAT/GST compliance for each market

## Building a Maintenance Calendar

| Frequency | Tasks |
|---|---|
| **Weekly** | Apply plugin/theme updates in staging, push to production; review order anomalies |
| **Monthly** | Review site speed, check broken links, audit top product pages |
| **Quarterly** | Security audit, review marketing performance vs. KPIs, update policies if needed |
| **Annually** | Full platform review — evaluate whether hosting, plugins, and theme still meet needs |

<Quiz question="Your eCommerce site has been running for two years without a dedicated maintenance plan. What are the three highest-risk areas you would address first, and what would 'addressed' look like for each?">

<div>

(1) **Security** — Two years of unmanaged updates means accumulated vulnerabilities. Addressed means: run a security scan (Wordfence or Sucuri) to identify current issues, update all plugins/themes/core in a staging environment and push to production, change all admin passwords, and establish a recurring update schedule. (2) **Backups** — Without a backup plan, any server failure or hack could result in permanent data loss. Addressed means: a daily automated backup is running to an offsite location, and a test restore has been successfully completed. (3) **Performance** — Sites degrade over time as databases grow, images accumulate, and plugins compound. Addressed means: run a PageSpeed audit, implement or verify caching is active, optimize the database, and set a monthly performance review reminder. These three form the foundation that everything else depends on — without them, marketing investment is at risk of being wiped out by a preventable outage or breach.
