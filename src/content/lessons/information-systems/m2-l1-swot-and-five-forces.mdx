## Why Strategy Tools Matter in IS

Information systems don't exist in a vacuum — they are built and deployed to support business strategy. Before an organization can decide which IS to invest in, it needs to understand its competitive environment and internal capabilities. That's where strategic analysis frameworks come in.

---

## SWOT Analysis

**SWOT** stands for **Strengths, Weaknesses, Opportunities, Threats**. It's a structured way to assess an organization's internal position and external environment.

| | Internal | External |
|---|---|---|
| **Positive** | Strengths | Opportunities |
| **Negative** | Weaknesses | Threats |

**Strengths and Weaknesses** are internal — things the organization controls. **Opportunities and Threats** are external — things in the environment the organization must respond to.

### Example: A Regional Bank Considering a Mobile Banking App

- **Strength** — Established customer trust, existing branch infrastructure
- **Weakness** — Legacy core banking system, slow IT development cycle
- **Opportunity** — Growing mobile banking adoption among younger customers
- **Threat** — Fintech startups offering superior digital experiences with no legacy costs

The SWOT doesn't tell you what to do — it tells you what questions to answer. The strategic insight comes from matching strengths to opportunities and addressing weaknesses before threats exploit them.

---

## Porter's Five Forces

Developed by Michael Porter, the **Five Forces** framework analyzes the competitive intensity of an industry:

1. **Threat of New Entrants** — How easily can new competitors enter the market?
2. **Bargaining Power of Suppliers** — How much leverage do suppliers have over price and terms?
3. **Bargaining Power of Buyers** — How much leverage do customers have?
4. **Threat of Substitutes** — Can customers get the same value another way?
5. **Rivalry Among Existing Competitors** — How intense is competition between current players?

### IS Implication

Technology disrupts Five Forces. Cloud computing lowered the barrier to entry for software companies (Threat of New Entrants increased). E-commerce shifted Bargaining Power of Buyers dramatically — price comparison is now instant. Understanding how IS changes competitive forces is a core IS management skill.

<Pitfall title="SWOT Is Not a Strategy">

A completed SWOT matrix is an input to strategy, not the strategy itself. Many teams fill out a SWOT and stop there. The value comes from the conversation after: "Given this SWOT, what should we prioritize and why?" Without that synthesis step, SWOT is just a list.

</Pitfall>
