## The AI You Know

Most people's first experience with AI is invisible. A chatbot answers your question. A recommendation engine suggests a show. A spam filter silently deletes an email.

These systems live entirely inside computers. They take text in, produce text out. Nothing in the physical world changes because of them — unless a human reads the output and decides to act.

That's about to change.

---

## When AI Grows a Body

**Physical AI** is the application of artificial intelligence to systems that interact with the real world — systems that move, manipulate, navigate, and respond to physical conditions.

The key difference from software AI:

| Software AI | Physical AI |
|---|---|
| Acts on data | Acts on the world |
| Mistakes are usually reversible | Mistakes can cause physical harm |
| Runs in a controlled environment | Operates in an unpredictable world |
| Can pause and restart | Must respond in real time |
| Failure = wrong answer | Failure = collision, injury, damage |

A chatbot that gives bad advice is a problem. A self-driving car that makes a bad decision at 60 mph is a catastrophe.

---

## Three Things That Make It Hard

Physical AI faces challenges that pure software AI doesn't:

**1. The real world doesn't wait.**
Software can process a query in its own time. A robot arm moving toward a human worker has milliseconds to detect a collision and stop. Latency isn't a user experience issue — it's a safety issue.

**2. Sensors lie.**
Cameras get dirty. LiDAR gets confused by rain. GPS fails indoors. Physical AI systems must work with noisy, incomplete, and sometimes wrong data about the world they're operating in.

**3. Actions have consequences.**
You can't "undo" a surgical incision or a warehouse collision. Physical AI must be right — or at least safely wrong — in a way that software never has to be.

---

## Why Now?

Physical AI isn't new. Factories have used robotic arms since the 1960s. What's changed:

- **Compute** — AI chips are now small enough and cheap enough to embed in devices
- **Data** — decades of sensor data and video have trained perception models that actually work
- **ML advances** — deep learning cracked computer vision, the hardest problem in physical AI
- **Cost** — sensors that cost $75,000 in 2012 cost $500 today

The result: physical AI is moving out of controlled factory floors and into hospitals, roads, farms, and homes.
