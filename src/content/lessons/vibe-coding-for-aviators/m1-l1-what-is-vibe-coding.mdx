## ✈️ Before You Take Off

You don't need to know how to code for this workshop. You need a Google account, a browser, and an idea worth building. That's it.

Over the next three days you'll describe small web apps in plain English to **Gemini**, and it will write the real HTML, CSS, and JavaScript for you — with a live preview right next to the conversation. This way of building has a name: **vibe coding**. You describe the *vibe* of what you want, the AI handles the syntax, and you steer by testing and refining until it feels right.

Before we open a laptop: **what's your dream aircraft or route you'd love to fly one day?** Turn to the person next to you and swap answers. You'll be paired up for build time over the next three days, so this is also your first handshake with a teammate.

## 🎛️ Your Cockpit: Gemini + Canvas

Everything you build this week happens inside one browser tab.

| What | Why it matters |
|---|---|
| **gemini.google.com**, Canvas turned on | You describe an app, Gemini writes the code, and shows you a live, working preview — all in the same window |
| A Google account | Free, and all you need to sign in. No installs, no code editor |
| A laptop or Chromebook + Chrome | That's the whole hardware list |

**If the live preview ever misbehaves:** copy Gemini's code into a plain text file, save it with a `.html` ending, and double-click it — it opens straight in Chrome. That's your parachute; you'll never be stuck.

<Pitfall title="Waiting for the 'Right' Idea">

The most common way to lose ten minutes on Day 1 is staring at a blank prompt box waiting for the perfect idea. Don't. Vibe coding rewards *starting* — you'll rebuild, rename, and redirect your project constantly over the next three days. Your first idea only has to be good enough to click "build."

</Pitfall>

## 🗺️ The Flight Plan: Three Days, One Skill Stack

Each day adds one new layer of thinking on top of the same core skill — prompting Gemini to build something. Nothing gets re-taught from scratch; it stacks.

| Day | New layer | In practice |
|---|---|---|
| **1 — Today** | The Vibe Coding Loop | A repeatable process for turning an idea into working code |
| **2** | Think Like a Builder | Who it's for, what it must do well, and three UX basics |
| **3** | Ship It | Real peer feedback, one honest iteration, and a demo pitch |

By Friday afternoon, every one of you will have a small folder of real, working projects you built yourself — and a process you can reuse for the next one, whether that's a flight-planning tool, a checklist app, or something that has nothing to do with aviation at all.

<Quiz question="You open Gemini and stare at a blank prompt box because you're not sure your app idea is good enough yet. What's the better move?">
<div>

**Start building anyway — a rough first attempt beats a perfect idea you never typed.**

Vibe coding is a loop, not a one-shot guess. You'll test what you build, notice what's off, and refine it — probably several times before the day is over. The value isn't in nailing the idea on the first try; it's in getting *something* on screen fast enough that you have material to react to.

</div>
</Quiz>
