## 🔁 Recap: Two Days, Two Lenses

Day 1 gave you the loop. Day 2 gave you the PM and UX lenses — who it's for, what it must do well, and three habits that make it usable. Today adds the most important lens of all: **someone else's eyes.**

Here's the thing nobody tells you until they've built something: **you cannot see your own project the way a stranger sees it.** You already know where every button is and what it's supposed to do. A first-time user doesn't. The gap between those two experiences is exactly what today fixes.

**Rose** — what worked. Be specific: not "I liked it," but "the button color made it obvious what to click first."

**Thorn** — what confused them. Also specific: not "it's fine," but "I didn't know I could click on the aircraft image."

A rose without a thorn is just a compliment. A thorn without a rose feels like criticism. You need both to actually know what to keep and what to fix.

<Pitfall title="Defending Your Build During Testing">

When your partner hits a confusing spot, the instinct is to jump in and explain: *"Oh, you're supposed to click there first."* Don't. If you have to explain it, that's the thorn — write it down instead of talking them through it. The two minutes only teach you something if you stay silent and just watch.

</Pitfall>

## 🧪 Today's Test

Pick whichever project from this week you want to improve — Day 1's boarding pass, Day 2's aircraft quiz, or something new. Trade with a partner:

1. Hand them your project with **zero explanation**
2. They use it cold for **2 minutes**, out loud if possible (narrating what they're thinking helps you both)
3. They give you **one rose** and **one thorn**
4. Switch — now you test theirs the same way

Write both pieces of feedback down. You'll use them in the next lesson.

**The colors are the rose (keep it) — the unclear clickable plane image is the thorn (that's your next fix).**

The thorn tells you exactly where clarity or feedback is missing — in this case, the image doesn't look clickable, so hierarchy or a hover state could fix it.
