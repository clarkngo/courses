## 🔁 Vibe Coding Is a Conversation

Here's the part beginners get wrong most often: they think vibe coding is *one prompt, then walk away*. It isn't. You work together with the AI in a loop — describe, test, refine, repeat — and the app gets better with every exchange.

| Step | What happens |
|---|---|
| **1. Describe** (The Spark) | Tell the AI what you want to build |
| **2. Test** (The Takeoff) | Run the app. Click the buttons. What works? What feels off? |
| **3. Refine** (The Adjustment) | Tell the AI exactly how to improve it |
| **4. Repeat** | Watch your app get better with every exchange |

---

## 🧮 Walking Through the Loop

Let's make this concrete with one example, start to finish.

**Step 1 — Describe:**
> "Make me a simple flight fuel calculator with a clean blue theme."

The AI writes the working app: input fields for distance and burn rate, a calculate button, a result showing total fuel needed, styled in blue.

**Step 2 — Test:**
You open it. You type in some numbers, hit calculate — it works. But you notice two things: nothing tells you if the button was actually clicked, and there's no warning if the fuel amount looks dangerously low.

**Step 3 — Refine:**
> "That's great! Now make the button turn green when clicked, and add a warning if the fuel is too low."

**Step 4 — Repeat:**
The AI updates the app. You test again. Maybe the warning threshold is wrong, so you refine again: *"Make the warning trigger under 50 gallons, not 10."* Loop again. Each pass, the app gets closer to what you actually pictured.

Nothing about this loop requires you to know *how* the button color changes in code, or *how* the warning logic gets written. You only ever describe outcomes and react to what you see.

---

## 🎯 Why the Loop Matters

The loop is the whole method. There's no separate "advanced" technique waiting for you later — professionals doing this at a much bigger scale are running the exact same four steps, just with bigger ideas and more rounds of refinement.

<Quiz question="You describe an app, and the first version isn't quite right — a button doesn't do what you expected. What's the correct next move in the loop?">

**Refine: tell the AI specifically what's wrong and what you want instead, then test again.**

The first version is rarely the final version, and that's normal — it's not a sign you did something wrong. Refining based on what you noticed during testing is step 3 of the loop, not a detour from it.
