## 🚀 Start Simple

You don't need a big idea for your first build — you need a small one you can finish today. Good starter projects:

- An **interactive quiz** on a topic you know well
- A **custom dashboard** tracking something you care about (habits, a budget, a reading list)
- A **personal landing page** introducing yourself or a project

Each of these is small enough to finish in one sitting, and real enough to actually use afterward.

---

## 🧠 Trust Your Ideas

The tech is no longer the obstacle. Whatever you can clearly describe, a modern AI coding assistant — ChatGPT, Claude, Gemini, or similar — can turn into working code. Your imagination is the only limit now, not your technical background.

---

## 📝 Your Fill-in-the-Blank Starter Prompt

Open an AI chat tool that can write and run code, and adapt this template:

<CodeLab title="Fill-in-the-Blank Starter Prompt">

```
Make me a [type of app] that lets me [main action]. It should
have [specific features], and use a [color/style] theme.
```

**Example — a habit tracker:**

```
Make me a habit tracker that lets me check off habits each day.
It should have a list of 5 habits with checkboxes and a streak
counter, and use a clean green theme.
```

Every blank above is filled with something *specific* — not "some habits," but "a list of 5 habits with checkboxes," not "nice colors," but "a clean green theme." That specificity is Golden Rule 1 in action.

</CodeLab>

Paste your filled-in version in, and you'll have a working first draft in seconds. From there, you're already in the loop from Module 3: test it, notice what's off, and refine.

---

## 🎯 Your Mission

1. Pick one small idea from the list above (or your own).
2. Fill in the starter prompt template and send it.
3. Test what comes back — click everything.
4. Refine at least twice, using the "what's broken + when + fix it" pattern from the Golden Rules.
