## 💡 The Spark

In early 2025, AI scientist **Andrej Karpathy** noticed something: people were building genuinely impressive, working apps without ever looking at a single line of code. They'd describe what they wanted, let the AI write it, test the result, and describe the next tweak — over and over — without reading or writing code themselves.

He gave that behavior a name: **"vibe coding"** — fully leaning into the creative flow and letting the AI handle the gears under the hood. The term stuck because it captured something real: the *feel* of building shifted from mechanical to conversational.

---

## 🌍 Today: A Movement, Not a Trick

Vibe coding didn't stay a novelty. It's now a massive, ongoing movement:

- **Creators** build tools for their own workflows
- **Artists** build interactive pieces without touching a framework
- **Teachers** build classroom tools tailored to their exact lesson plans
- **Students** build the exact study tool they wish already existed

Every single day, people who would never call themselves "programmers" are shipping working software. The label "programmer" used to be the gate. Now it isn't.

<Pitfall title="Assuming 'Vibe Coding' Is Just a Buzzword">
It's tempting to hear "vibe coding" and assume it's marketing spin for something that already existed. It isn't quite — the *ability* to describe an app in plain English and get working code back is genuinely new, made possible by AI models becoming good enough to reliably translate intent into correct code. The name is new; so is the capability it's naming.
</Pitfall>

<Quiz question="Why did Karpathy's term 'vibe coding' catch on instead of fading as internet slang usually does?">
<div>

**Because it named a real, new capability — building working software by describing it, without reading or writing code — not just a new attitude toward old tools.**

Catchy names fade when there's nothing new underneath them. This one stuck because thousands of people were independently doing exactly what the term described, and needed a word for it.

</div>
</Quiz>
