## Adapters

Adapters are fittings with **connection threads on two or more sides**. They are found in practically all sizes, styles, and thread types.

### Five Uses of Adapters

1. **Change thread type** — e.g., from JIC to O-Ring Boss in an in-line port connection
2. **Jump size** — reduce or enlarge the connection or hose line size
3. **Port connection with straight hose fitting** — eliminates stress on port and coupling; looks more streamlined
4. **Simplify orientation** — use a bent tube flange adapter instead of two bent tubes; faster to make and install
5. **Tee or Cross** — accommodate several lines into a common port

> **Always install the adapter first, then the hose assembly.**

### Conversion Adapters

Gates carries a full line of **International Conversion Adapters** for connecting SAE couplings to global equipment — for example, mounting a tool with SAE threads onto British equipment.

### When Using Adapters — 5 Application Rules

1. Use to avoid fitting orientation when an angle fitting is needed on both ends (use angle on one end, straight + angle adapter on the other)
2. Use to make a size jump when jump-size fittings aren't available
3. Use to ease port connection and hose installation
4. Use to change to a different thread configuration, including international threads
5. As a rule of thumb: **a straight adapter + bent tube coupling provides better flow and less pressure drop than an angled adapter + straight hose end**

> Adapters can be a **potential leak point** — use them purposefully, not casually.

## Proper Hose Assembly

Once you select the right hose and couplings, you make the assembly. There are three types:
- **Permanent Crimped**
- **Permanent Swage**
- **Field Attachable**

---

## Step 1 — Measuring the Hose

Understanding the difference between **cut hose length** and **overall assembly length** is critical.

![Diagram showing Assembly Overall Length = Cut Hose Length + C1 (left coupling) + C2 (right coupling). The "C" value is the portion of each coupling that extends outside the hose.](/courses/images/hydraulic-hose-fittings/m5/assembly-length.png)

**Formula:**
> **Cut Hose Length = Assembly Overall Length − (C1 + C2)**

The **"C" cutoff value** is the length of the coupling that is NOT inside the hose. Subtract both C values from the overall assembly length to get your hose cut length. All C values are in the Gates Hydraulics Catalog coupling tables.

**Practical method (when no exact fit needed):**
Lay the old hose on a bench, align the new fittings with the old fitting ends, and measure the distance between the locking grooves on the new fittings.

### SAE Length Tolerances

| Hose Length | Tolerance |
|-------------|-----------|
| 0" to 12" | ±1/8" |
| 12" to 18" | ±3/16" |
| 18" to 36" | ±1/4" |
| Above 36" | ±1" |

*Length measured to the nearest 1/8".*

## Step 2 — Cutting the Hose

Use a **cutoff saw**. There are two blade types:

| Blade | Use | Notes |
|-------|-----|-------|
| **Notched (serrated)** | Non-spiral hose (braided, textile) | Won't work well on spiral hose; dulls quickly |
| **Abrasive wheel** | All hose types including spiral | Creates more debris; diameter shrinks with wear |

> **Keep the cut straight and square.** Maximum allowable cut angle is **5°**.

**For PTFE (C14) hose:** Wrap with masking tape, cut with abrasive wheel or cutting shear, de-burr the PTFE tube with a sharp knife, then remove tape before assembly.

---

## Step 3 — Cleaning the Hose

Cutting generates debris that WILL enter and damage the hydraulic system if not removed.

Three cleaning methods (least to most effective):

1. **Blow shop air** through the complete assembly — easiest but least effective
2. **Soak a sponge** in water and isopropyl alcohol; push through hose with shop air; repeat until clean
3. **Fluid flushing apparatus** — flush cleaning fluid at high velocity; most effective

---

## Step 4 — Hose Preparation (Skiving & Buffing)

### Skiving
Skiving removes the hose cover **down to the reinforcement** so the ferrule serrations can grip the wire. Required when the cover is too thick for the ferrule to bite through.

- Tools: wire abrasion wheel or **Gates Hand Skiving Tool Kit (Part No. 78030)**
- **Skive length** = length of cover removed
- **Skive diameter** = diameter after skiving (e.g., "to the wire")
- Hoses with **thin covers** do NOT need skiving

> ⚠️ Always wear **safety glasses** when skiving. Wear hearing protection when power-skiving.

### Buffing
Similar to skiving but does NOT remove cover all the way to the reinforcement — only to a specific diameter from the crimp data chart.

- Use a **grinding wheel** (never a wire wheel — it damages reinforcement)
- Required for some non-wire reinforced hoses

---

## Step 5 — Coupling Preparation

### Two-Piece Fittings (PC, PCS, PCM, GS)

1. Lubricate the first 2–3 serrations on the stem with lightweight lubricant
2. Clamp stem in a vise on the hex portion; push the hose onto the stem until it bottoms out against the stem shoulder
3. Check full insertion: pull the ferrule down — the stem should be level with the top of the ferrule
4. Push the ferrule so it rests against the hex of the stem — ready for crimping

### One-Piece Fittings (MegaCrimp®)

1. Place the hose next to the coupling; mark the insertion depth with a marker or grease pencil
2. Push the coupling until the shell aligns with the mark; twist slightly to confirm full insertion
3. Use the **Gates MegaCrimp® Coupling Insertion Tool (Part No. 78017)** to ensure proper depth

### Fitting Orientation

When both fittings are bent tubes or block style, they must be oriented to minimize hose twist:

1. Position the far coupling **vertically downward**
2. Measure orientation angle **clockwise**
3. Tolerance: **±2°**

---

## Step 6 — Basic Crimp Procedures

1. Consult the **crimp data chart** for: ferrule selection, die selection, approximate crimp setting, finished crimp diameter, skive/buff data
2. Load selected dies into the crimper; apply thin lubricant to the die contact surface and cone (NOT the bore) on first use
3. Adjust the machine to the proper crimp setting
4. Insert the hose assembly and align the coupling with the die fingers
5. Install die cone if used
6. **Wear safety glasses; keep hands and clothing away from moving parts**
7. Activate the crimping mechanism
8. Remove and **measure the final crimp diameter**

> ⚠️ **Always measure the final crimp diameter.** All settings are approximate. If the crimp is out of tolerance, recalibration or component replacement may be required.

---

## Step 7 — Measuring Crimp Diameters

![Two sketches: Sketch 1 shows a caliper measuring crimp diameter between ridges halfway along the ferrule. Sketch 2 shows measuring halfway down the crimped portion of the ferrule, with the code identification mark visible.](/courses/images/hydraulic-hose-fittings/m5/crimp-measurement.png)

- **Sketch 1:** Measure between ridges, halfway between top and bottom of coupling. Caliper fingers must NOT touch the ridges.
- **Sketch 2:** Measure halfway down the crimped portion of the ferrule.
- For small crimp diameters (3/16", 3/8", 1/4"), use jaw-type micrometers or the **Gates Digital Caliper (Part No. 78241)**.

---

## Permanent Swage — C14 (PTFE)

For PTFE hose assemblies, a swager (not a crimper) is used. Basic steps:

1. Insert correct die and pusher per Gates Swage Data Chart
2. Lubricate inner bore surfaces of dies with thin lightweight oil
3. Feed hose assembly through dies; hold hose and coupling into the pusher
4. Roll control lever guiding coupling into die until pusher bottoms against die surface
5. Push control lever to retract pusher; open die halves; remove assembly

> ⚠️ **Use swagers only if you:** have received hands-on training, follow current Gates Operating Manual & Swage Data, use only NEW (unused) Gates hose and fittings, and wear SAFETY GLASSES.

---

## Field Attachable (Reusable) — "Type T" Assembly

No crimper needed. Install with a vise and wrenches:

1. **Thoroughly lubricate** the stem of the coupling
2. Place socket in vise; turning **clockwise**, thread hose into socket until it bottoms; turn back **one-half turn**
3. In a clockwise motion, thread the stem into the hose and socket until the **stem hex shoulders against the ferrule**
