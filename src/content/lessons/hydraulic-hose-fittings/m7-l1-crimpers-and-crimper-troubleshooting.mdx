## How a Crimper Works

A crimper is a **hydraulic ram**. Here's how it operates:

![Crimper schematic showing: Motor → Pump → Reservoir (Tank); fluid flows to Cylinder extending Piston → Rod → Pusher down onto Tapered Die Cone → Die Cone forces Dies together → crimps the fitting](/courses/images/hydraulic-hose-fittings/m7/crimper-diagram.png)

1. The **pump** pressurizes fluid
2. Pressurized fluid flows to the **cylinder**, extending the piston
3. The piston drives the **rod** and **pusher** down onto the **tapered die cone**
4. The die cone's taper forces the **dies together**, crimping the fitting onto the hose

Crimpers come in many configurations — vertical (bottom- or top-loading), horizontal, or angled. The operating principle is the same regardless of orientation.

| Pump Type | Maintenance Requirement |
|-----------|------------------------|
| **Electric over hydraulic** | Check fluid level monthly |
| **Air over hydraulic** | 1–2 drops of oil in air chamber daily; check fluid level monthly |
| **Hand operated** | Check fluid level monthly |

## Crimper Set-Up

1. Remove the crimper from its shipping container
2. Position on a **stable surface**
3. Connect the hydraulic hose assembly to the crimper and pump (refer to pump manual for port connection)
4. Install the breather cap in the reservoir tank if needed
5. Make the proper electrical connections
6. **Bleed any air from the system** (follow procedures in the crimper operating manual)
7. **Lubricate all metal-to-metal sliding surfaces** (dies, cones, die shoes) with the recommended lubricant
8. Check calibration; re-calibrate as required
9. Mail in the equipment registration form — ensures updated crimp data is forwarded to you

## Crimper Maintenance

Proper maintenance keeps a crimper accurate and safe. Poorly maintained crimpers produce **poorly-performing assemblies that can blow apart and cause injury**.

| Maintenance Task | Detail |
|-----------------|--------|
| **Lubrication** | Reapply lubricant to metal-to-metal sliding surfaces when surfaces become shiny |
| **Calibration** | Check every month — especially when you notice a change in crimp diameter |
| **Hose assembly** | Check the hose connecting pump to cylinder; replace if needed with a properly-rated assembly |
| **Fluid level** | Keep within ½" from top of tank opening; prevents air from entering the system |
| **Die wear and storage** | Inspect for indentations, chips, cracks, gouges; store in racks or original packaging |
| **Cleaning** | Remove debris with a clean shop rag — dirt contaminates fluid and damages crimp components |
| **Oil change and bleeding** | Change fluid periodically; fluid viscosity degrades under heat and high usage |

## Crimper Troubleshooting

The **three most common crimper problem categories** are:
1. **Cylinder seal** — seals between piston and cylinder may leak or roll over time
2. **Electrical** — power source compatibility issues; wiring problems
3. **Pump** — debris in fluid causing stuck valves; cold temperature thickening fluid; air in system

---

## PowerCrimp® 707 Troubleshooting

| Problem | Solution |
|---------|---------|
| **Ram stuck, won't return** | Slowly loosen the hose at the pump (catch escaping fluid); if fluid escapes, the check valve may be stuck — contact Gates rep; if no fluid escapes, replace piston seal with proper seal kit |
| **Digital display is blank** | Check all wiring connections — wrong voltage will damage the display; replace if needed |
| **Digital display is incorrect** | Check electrical connections including back of display; check calibration; replace switch box |
| **Cylinder leaking fluid** | Disassemble per seal replacement instructions; check cylinder wall for scoring; if damaged, replace cylinder; if not, replace worn seal |
| **Broken die fingers or cone** | Check crimp specs (proper die, hose, cone, settings); check calibration; replace broken parts. NOTE: Never drop dies — dropping can break them or damage the cone |
| **Crimper won't plug into power unit** | Check voltage rating on both pump and crimper — 115-volt and 230-volt connectors differ; both must match |
| **Over- or under-crimping** | Check calibration per operating manual; adjust actuator rod or switch box position; check crimp specifications |

> ⚠️ **Voltage Warning:** If the PowerCrimp® 707 pump is changed to 230V, a new 230V cord assembly (78748) must be installed at the same time. If the new cord is not used, the 230-volt supply will burn out the digital readout within minutes.

## OmniCrimp® 21 Troubleshooting

| Problem | Solution |
|---------|---------|
| **Numbers won't display on digital readout** | Turn the JOG switch to reverse position; once RAM recycles, readout is ready |
| **Dies won't stay in die shoes** | Check and adjust the **detente** — must be just above the base of the die shoe. Use the detente wrench (inside die shoe grooves): clockwise to lower, counter-clockwise to raise |
| **Pump will not shut off** | Faulty computer panel — return to manufacturer; contact Gates Application Engineer for instructions |
| **Crimp OD out of spec** | The OmniCrimp 21 is pre-calibrated before shipping and should not need re-calibration. If OD is off, adjust the crimp setting on the crimp chart. If re-calibration is truly needed, contact Gates Application Engineer |

---

## MobileCrimp® 4-20 Troubleshooting

| Problem | Solution |
|---------|---------|
| **Ram will not fully extend** | Check hydraulic oil level; oil temperature must be between +40°F and +120°F |
| **Ram will not retract** | Unplug pump (safety first); carefully loosen hose at pump — if ram retracts, pump valve may be stuck or need replacing |
| **Pump motor will not start** | Check electrical connections |
| **Setting will not change** | Locking switch may be engaged — move switch to the left |
| **Light and buzzer do not work** | Replace controller batteries; refer to Maintenance section; replace controller if needed |

---

## GC32-XD™ Troubleshooting

| Problem | Solution |
|---------|---------|
| **Pump motor will not start** | Check electrical connections |
| **Setting will not change** | Locking switch may be engaged — move switch to the left |
| **Will not hold crimp calibration** | Flange head bolts are loose — check torque to **330 Nm (2,900 in-lbs)** every six months |
| **Red LED flashing rapidly** | Problem with electronics in control panel — refer to safety and operating manual |
