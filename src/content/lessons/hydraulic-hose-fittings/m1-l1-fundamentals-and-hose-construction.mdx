## Why Hydraulic Hose?

Early hydraulic systems used rigid pipes and tubes. Hydraulic hose replaced them because it offers significant practical advantages in real-world equipment.

A hose:

- Is **less costly** to manufacture and replace
- **Weighs less**, reducing overall equipment load
- **Absorbs shock and vibration** better than rigid pipe
- Is **easier to route and install** around complex machinery
- Requires **no brazing or specialized bending**
- **Allows movement** between components of equipment
- Is **less likely to rust**
- **Absorbs sound** better
- **Dampens pressure surges** in the system

> The international marketplace for hydraulic assemblies is growing every day — making this knowledge increasingly valuable across industries.

## Hose Construction

Every hydraulic hose is made up of **three core components**: the Tube, the Reinforcement, and the Cover. Each layer has a distinct role.

![Diagram of a hydraulic hose cross-section showing three layers: Tube (innermost), Reinforcement (middle), and Cover (outermost)](/courses/images/hydraulic-hose-fittings/m1/hose-construction-diagram.png)

---

### 1. The Tube

The **Tube** is the innermost layer — the conduit through which fluid flows.

Tube construction varies based on:
- **a) The materials being conveyed** — different fluids (oil, water-based, synthetic) require compatible tube materials
- **b) The temperature of the material** — extreme heat or cold affects which compound is used

**Key fact:** The **Inside Diameter (ID)** of the tube determines the size of the hose. Always size a hose by its ID, not its outside diameter.

---

### 2. The Reinforcement

The **Reinforcement** is the muscle of the hose. It provides the strength to resist internal pressure — or external pressure in suction/vacuum applications.

There are **three basic configurations**:

| Type | Description | Use Case |
|------|-------------|----------|
| **Braided** | Wire or textile, single or multiple layers | General hydraulic applications |
| **Spiraled** | Wire wound in 4 or 6 layers (plies) | Demanding, high-pressure applications; longer service life |
| **Helical** | Coil reinforcement | Prevents hose from collapsing during suction/vacuum |

![Three hose cross-sections showing braided, spiraled, and helical reinforcement types side by side](/courses/images/hydraulic-hose-fittings/m1/reinforcement-types.png)

> Hoses with multiple reinforcement layers have an **anti-friction layer** between them to prevent steel wires from rubbing against each other — extending hose life.

---

### 3. The Cover

The **Cover** is the outermost layer. It protects both the reinforcement and the tube from environmental damage.

The cover shields the hose from:

- Weather
- Ozone
- Abrasion
- Corrosion
- Temperature extremes
- Chemicals

---

## Key Takeaway

A hydraulic hose is not a simple tube — it's a precisely engineered assembly. The **tube** carries the fluid, the **reinforcement** handles the pressure, and the **cover** protects it all from the outside world. Understanding these layers helps you select the right hose and identify failure causes.
